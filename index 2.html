About

Although currently based in Frederick County, MD, I was raised in the nether realm of rural Minnesota. In those halcyon days I apprenticed in the ancient and deadly arts of growing corn and driving stick. After high school I shipped off to study analytic philosophy and sociology at the University of Minnesota Duluth. I mowed lawns and planted flowers for a living. Subsequently, I lived in New York for two years while obtaining an MA in Sociology from the New School for Social Research. My coursework emphasized historical approaches within sociology, as well as the histories of sociological and political thought.

Far less useless than it sounds, philosophy taught me to read things 5 or 6 times if I didn't understand them.

The pay-off came when I started struggling with the documentation for JavaScript.

My current interests have traveled quite far afield from the days when I could be sated by close readings of Lacan and Hegel - my life-long obsessive reading has come to fruition with my recent interest in typography and publication design. I also have taken an interest in visualizing social scientific data - I am working now to master the ancient and deadly arts of Ruby and JavaScript.

I am always eager both to learn new skills and develop existing ones. My general approach to work and to problem solving is guided by the idea that rather than muddling through a problem, a stitch in time saves nine. I am a firm believer that research and planning in the earliest stages of any project will save resources and prevent heartache in the future.

Sociology taught me how to analyze and understand data beyond glib bullet points.

Web
  • CONTENT STRATEGY
  • RESPONSIVE DESIGN
  • USER INTERFACE

In the early days, web design was simple: a relatively small amount of content designed for a single device. Firms can no longer rely on their audience accessing their content or services on desktop machines. I am of the belief that websites should be functional on mobile and desktop platforms alike. This is the baseline. The real challenge is making sites that are beautiful on mobile and desktop.

Responsive design works together with good content strategy. The presentation and the content must work together for optimal user experience.

Responsive design is the baseline.

Data
  • VISUALIZATION
  • ANALYSIS

Data analysis should consist of more than just a few bullet points and spreadsheets.

Data analysis should consist of more than just a few bullet points and spreadsheets. I am a broadly trained sociologist, with equal skill in qualitative and quantitative methods. High quality data analysis isn't just knowing what to do with numbers, it's knowing how much of the story they reveal.

Of course, data analysis isn't enough on it's own - humans are visual animals. Creating quality visualizations of data is absolutely necessary. Explanations are only as good as their presentation.

Design

Good design is also very important. It is important to keep a balance between following design trends and reinforcing homogeneity. The only thing worse than bland workmanship is a dated aesthetic.

My guiding rule is that frameworks like Boostrap or Foundation don't restrict design so much as they give the designer a set of working constraints from which beautiful design can emerge.
